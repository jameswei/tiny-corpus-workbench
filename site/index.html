This site describes the workbench. It does not process documents.

Purpose and workflow

One evidence-preserving lifecycle.

The workbench connects each document change to its source, evidence, decision, and resulting revision.

  1. 01 Original source Unchanged and identified
  2. 02 Extraction Format-aware outputs
  3. 03 Evidence-based diagnosis Findings cite document items
  4. 04 Explicit human decision Authority stays with people
  5. 05 Reversible refinement Operations remain attributable
  6. 06 Prepared revision Auditable output boundary
  7. 07 Corpus inspection and comparison Patterns across mixed formats

A local visual workbench connects sources, artifacts, findings, decisions, transformations, revisions, and comparisons without replacing the CLI or audit contracts.

One inspectable history

Four capabilities, one record.

Each capability reads or extends the same evidence trail. Raw artifacts, findings, decisions, and revisions stay connected.

01

Extract

Compare Docling and MarkItDown while retaining lossless canonical evidence and source identity.

02

Diagnose

Record deterministic findings with evidence, stable identifiers, severity, provenance, and affected items.

03

Refine

Require explicit decisions, create new prepared revisions, and preserve reversible operations with append-only history.

04

Compare

Inspect patterns across a mixed-format corpus through offline reports and a loopback-only visual workbench.

Trust and control

Preserve evidence. Keep people in control.

The preparation record stays inspectable, but evidence alone never grants authority to change a document.

Evidence stays inspectable

Retain the record.

Original sources, raw extraction artifacts, findings, revisions, hashes, and history are retained instead of silently overwritten.

People retain authority

Require explicit decisions.

Diagnosis does not authorize mutation. Interpretive refinements require explicit human confirmation.

Authenticity limit: integrity checking does not establish authorship or authenticity.

Project boundary

Stop at a prepared revision.

The workbench ends when an inspectable prepared document revision is ready. Chunking, embeddings, indexing, retrieval, generation, and RAG evaluation remain outside the boundary.
