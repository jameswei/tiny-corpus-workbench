This site describes the workbench. It does not process documents.

Purpose and workflow

One evidence-preserving lifecycle.

The workbench is designed to connect each document change to its source, evidence, decision, and resulting revision.

  1. 01 Original source Unchanged and identified
  2. 02 Extraction Format-aware outputs
  3. 03 Evidence-based diagnosis Findings cite document items
  4. 04 Explicit human decision Authority stays with people
  5. 05 Reversible refinement Attributable operations
  6. 06 Prepared revision Auditable output boundary
  7. 07 Corpus inspection and comparison Patterns across mixed formats

The runnable local path covers source capture, extraction observation, evidence-based diagnosis, and read-only verification. The same evidence model provides the foundation for controlled revisions, corpus comparison, and visual exploration.

One inspectable history

Build every capability on evidence.

Extraction and diagnosis establish one inspectable trail. Controlled revisions and corpus comparison extend the same product direction.

Trust and control

Preserve evidence. Keep people in control.

The current extraction and diagnosis records stay inspectable, but evidence alone never grants authority to change a document.

Evidence stays inspectable

Retain the record.

Original sources, raw extraction artifacts, findings, hashes, and verification evidence are retained instead of silently overwritten.

People retain authority

Require explicit decisions.

Diagnosis does not authorize mutation. Interpretive refinements require explicit human confirmation.

Authenticity limit: integrity checking does not establish authorship or authenticity.

Local by design

Run the implemented path locally.

The current CLI publishes extraction observations, deterministic diagnosis reports, and independent verification results. Start with extraction observation, then diagnose the canonical document without changing its evidence.

Project boundary

Stop at a prepared revision.

The workbench ends when an inspectable prepared document revision is ready. Chunking, embeddings, indexing, retrieval, generation, and RAG evaluation remain outside the boundary.
